What Your Birth Chart Reveals About Marriage

In Vedic astrology, marriage is primarily assessed through the 7th house (partnership and spouse) and its ruling planet, the 5th house (love and emotional connection), and the 2nd house (family and domestic life). Your current Dasha (planetary period) determines when marriage-related events are most likely to crystallise — and understanding this timing is often the single most useful thing a chart reading can offer.

  • Kundli Compatibility (Guna Milan)

    Traditional Guna Milan scores compatibility across eight dimensions — mental temperament, physical compatibility, emotional harmony, and long-term stability. The score is a useful starting point, but what matters more is how the charts interact at a deeper level: the 7th house lords, the Moon's placement, and whether the Dashas of both individuals are likely to align during the marriage period.

  • Marriage Timing

    Marriage doesn't happen randomly — it tends to occur during specific Dasha and transit periods that activate the 7th house. If marriage hasn't happened yet, the chart can indicate which upcoming periods are most likely to bring it. If multiple proposals have come and gone, the chart often shows why those periods weren't conducive even when everything else seemed right.

  • Manglik Dosha & Other Concerns

    Manglik Dosha — caused by Mars in certain houses — is one of the most widely misunderstood concepts in popular astrology. Its actual impact depends heavily on the house, the strength of Mars, the overall chart, and the partner's chart. Many people who've been told they are "strongly Manglik" have straightforward charts when properly analysed. An honest assessment prevents unnecessary anxiety and delays.

  • Navigating Difficulties in Marriage

    If a marriage is going through a difficult period — conflicts, distance, communication breakdowns — the chart often reflects the planetary periods that are creating friction. Understanding that a difficult phase is time-bound, and knowing when it is likely to ease, is valuable in ways that general relationship advice rarely is. It doesn't replace communication or effort, but it gives context.
